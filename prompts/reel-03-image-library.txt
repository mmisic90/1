A long shelf-lined corridor of legal volumes inside a private law
office library. Tall floor-to-ceiling shelves on both sides packed
with leather-bound legal books, gold-embossed spines catching pools
of warm tungsten light against deep navy painted walls and dark
walnut shelving. Strict one-point perspective. Narrow passage
between shelves runs straight to a single distant pool of warm
light at the vanishing point — a brass reading lamp on a small
polished walnut table. No people. The space feels infinite.

Between bookcase sections on each side wall, two large ornate
gilt-framed oil-painting portraits hang at eye level — one on the
left wall, one on the right wall. Both portraits depict distinguished
19th/early-20th century South Slavic legal scholars in dark formal
attire, classical academic oil portrait style, baroque gilded frames
catching warm tungsten light. (In post-production these placeholder
portraits will be replaced with public-domain photographs of
Valtazar Bogišić, 1834-1908, author of the General Property Code
for Montenegro, and Slobodan Jovanović, 1869-1958, constitutional
law theorist.)

Composition: vertical 9:16. Vanishing point at exact center
horizontally, slightly above geometric center vertically. Shelves
and portraits frame the corridor symmetrically. Distant lamp light
is small, gold, a point.

Style: cinematic, photorealistic, navy blue and gold color palette,
moody pooled tungsten light, deep shadows in the foreground,
shallow depth of field, Kubrick symmetry, professional editorial,
9:16 vertical, high detail, leather and gold-leaf book spines
visible, no readable book titles, no logos, no watermarks.

Reference cues: The Long Goodbye library scenes, Beauty and the
Beast library, Trinity College Long Room atmosphere.
